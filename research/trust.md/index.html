 Trustworthy ML & Robust Statistics

Statistical learning is an area of statistics initially demanded by enormous applications in agriculture and industry. However, its scope expanded after computer adventure and exhibition of big data as the goal was interpreting the underlying structure of the data. Statistical learning is categorized into two main groups including supervised learning where we know about labels of data –and we aim to predict an outcome measure– and unsupervised learning where we don’t know the labels and we want to interpret the data. Classification models are examples of supervised learning while the model is trying to predict a measure by pulling out a homogeneous pattern from data and fitting an appropriate model accordingly. Yet, regression models are used to understand unlabeled data without specifying an outcome measure. So these models train based on minimizing residual sum of squares to find out appropriate parameters as representative knubs of the model. Our goal in this lab is to develope suitable statistical learning methods for complicated types of data and improve the computational complexity of the methods because it is essential for big data.

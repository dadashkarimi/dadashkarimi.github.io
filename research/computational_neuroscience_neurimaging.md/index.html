 Computational Neuroscience & Neuroimaging

For the first time, we challenged neuroscience’s long-standing assumption that there is a single brain map that works for everyone across time. We looked at brain images acquired by functional Magnetic Resonance Imaging or fMRI, and tried to understand what brain regions talk to each other and how this conversation changes as we engage in different tasks, such as playing a game or watching a movie. We developed an artificial intelligence technology to automatically translate these brain images into brain maps, like the ones you can see here. We applied this technology to brain images of 718 individuals and constructed their brain maps as they performed 8 different tasks.

We observed that these brain maps are unique to each individual, such that we could uniquely identify people with 99% accuracy just by looking at their brain maps! The brain maps are like fingerprints! We also discovered that these brain maps are not fixed, but they reliably reconfigure by task, such that we could predict what people are doing, with up to 97% accuracy, just by looking at their brain maps!
